Liam Neeson | Early Life

Liam Neeson was born Liam John Neeson in Ballymena, County Antrim, Northern Ireland, on June 7, 1952. His mother, Katherine, worked as a cook, while Bernard’s father was a primary school caretaker.

Liam grew up in a Roman Catholic family with three sisters (Elizabeth, Bernadette, and Rosaleen) and began boxing classes at nine. He boxed until he was 17 years old, winning multiple regional titles along the way.

After a theater instructor offered him the lead role in a school play opposite a girl he had a crush on when he was 11 years old, Neeson became interested in acting.

He continued to perform throughout high school before enrolling at Queen’s University Belfast to study physics and computer science for a short time.

Liam Neeson | Career

After graduating from university, Neeson returned to Ballymena, where he worked in several part-time occupations, including forklift operator and truck driver. Neeson joined Belfast’s Lyric Players Theatre in 1976 and performed there for two years.

In 1980, Neeson was offered a small role in the Arthurian film ‘Excalibur‘ as Sir Gawain. Following the completion of the film, he relocated to London to pursue a career in theatre.

Between 1982 and 1987, he acted alongside Mel Gibson and Anthony Hopkins in “The Bounty” and Robert De Niro in “The Mission.”

In 2005, Neeson starred in the film “Batman Begins,” then from 2006 to 2011, he appeared in films such as “Seraphim Falls,” “Unknown,” “The Grey,” and “Clash of the Titans.”

Moreover, he worked on ‘The Dark Knight Rises,’ the final installment in the Batman film trilogy and the sequel to ‘Batman Begins‘ and ‘The Dark Knight‘ a few years later.

In 2014, Neeson featured in the film adaptation of the best-selling novel of the same name, titled “A Walk Among the Tombstones.”

Also, he voiced roles in several films, including ‘The Simpsons,’ ‘The Chronicles of Narnia,’ ‘Fallout 3‘, and ‘Star Wars: The Clone Wars.’
